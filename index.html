A conversational hybrid book recommender.

Type what you want — "something like Dune but lighter", "a short mystery set in Japan" — and BookDB parses your intent, then ranks books with a hybrid of item-based collaborative filtering and TF-IDF/LSA semantic search, fused by reciprocal-rank fusion. Every pick comes with the reasons why.

01 — How it works

Three signals, one ranked list.

Collaborative filtering and content similarity find different correct books. Reciprocal-rank fusion rewards a book ranked highly by either, so the fused list captures the union — and beats both signals alone.

🤝

Collaborative filtering

Item-based CF: mean-centered cosine over a user×book ratings matrix, scoring candidates by similarity-weighted votes from your seed books.

discovery/collab.py
🧭

Semantic search

TF-IDF (1–2 grams) + LSA (TruncatedSVD) over each book's title, genres, tags and description; cosine similarity to your free text or a seed book.

discovery/semantic.py
🔀

RRF fusion

Reciprocal-rank fusion of the CF and semantic rankings, weighted by alpha. A weighted-score blend is also available.

discovery/hybrid.py
💬

Conversational parser

A transparent rule/keyword parser — no LLM. Extracts seed book, genre/mood/setting filters, and modifiers like "but lighter". Every field is traceable.

discovery/query.py
📖

Explains itself

Each recommendation reports its CF and semantic component scores and a plain-English reason it was chosen.

Leave-one-out Hit-Rate@10 and MRR, comparing the hybrid against each single signal on full and sparse data.

02 — Evaluation

Does the hybrid actually help?

Leave-one-out: hold one of a reader's liked books out, seed the recommender with the rest, and check whether the held-out book lands in the top 10. Mean over 5 held-out draws, on the committed synthetic data. These are real demo.py numbers.

RankerFull data · Hit-Rate@10Sparse data (CF on 15%)
Collaborative filtering only0.6730.342
Semantic search only0.5500.515
Hybrid (RRF)0.6800.473

Full data: the hybrid beats both single signals. Sparse data: CF collapses when starved of ratings, but the content channel keeps the hybrid +0.13 ahead of CF — the hybrid is the only ranker that is never the worst. n = 357 readers, alpha = 0.6.

03 — Code & honest scope

Real, small, and tested.

Pure Python on numpy / scipy / scikit-learn / pandas. 30 pytest tests cover the parser, semantic topicality, the hybrid-beats-both result, and that "like X but <constraint>" respects the constraint. The "AI" is TF-IDF/LSA semantic search plus keyword/rule intent parsing plus CF fusion — no large language model. The 64-book catalog is hand-written; the 5,475 ratings are clearly-labeled synthetic.
